Portland Gourmet

A thoughtful, visual and interactive restaurant selection guide.

Introduction

Today's restaurants not only provide food to meet the needs of the taste buds, but also provide service and atmosphere to their customers to satisfy their inner needs. Restaurants’ atmospheres could not only be regarded as a competitive advantage of the restaurants as well as the determinants of customer satisfaction (Agoes and Pasaribu, 2015). Different atmospheres will attract different consumer groups.

In this project, we tried to explore the restaurant atmospheres in Portland, US. Through NLP methods and hierarchical clustering analysis, we identified several specific themes and also found significant spatial differences between different themes, which will help to learn the distribution of local consumer groups. With the analysis results, it might help the restaurant to better locate its atmosphere and improve its service quality. And from the perspective of cities, it could help the government to better plan the commercial facilities and improve safety supervision.

Analysis

Data collection, cleaning and cluster analysis.

Data COllection

Yelp is a popular online review community, which has been widely proved to have a great influence on consumers' decision-making (Ariyasriwatana and Quiroga, 2016). According to these reviews, restaurant customers could judge the reputation of the product and form their attitude to the product (Lim and van der Heide, 2015).Therefore, reviews can be regarded as records of customers' impression of the restaurant.  It could help to understand the theme and atmosphere of the restaurant by analyzing these comments.

The Yelp dataset (https://www.yelp.com/dataset) is a subset of businesses, reviews, and user data provided by Yelp website (Yelp, n.d.). Exploring Portland's 
restaurant data, there are 5644 restaurants in Portland with at least one yelp review, and 750395 reviews for those restaurants in total. However, due to computational limitations, we had to reduce the amount of reviews for NLP, so we selected only the recent 4 year's reviews, that leaves us 297993 reviews in Portland since Jan. 1st, 2017

About Portland

CIty overview

Portland is located at the confluence of the Willamette River into the Columbia River in Oregon, which is the biggest and most populous city in the U.S. state of Oregon, as well as a major port in the Pacific Northwest (City of Portland, 2021). As of July 2019, the United States Census Office counted 654,741 residents in Portland, grouped into 268,718 families. As a major port city and railroad hub in West Coast city of United States, convenient transportation has brought a constant stream of tourists to this city. Portland hosting 8.8 million visitors per year and spending over $5.3 billion directly (TravelPortalnd, 2021).

Urban spatial characteristics

The Portland neighborhoods can be divided into:
North: Major industrial area (heavy industry, manufacturing, logistics).
Northwest: one of Portland's most affluent neighborhoods, featuring upscale art galleries, high-end businesses, restaurants and commercial retail.
Southwest: primarily a single residential area for families. A small part of the southwest part of the downtown area.
Northeast: a mix of arts, commercial and residential areas. "Youth and cultural center".
East and Southeast: mixed residential areas with a high concentration of stores and apartments.

Focused Regional exploration

Schrock et al. (2015) indicate that Portland's food service industry exhibits a very clear tendency towards "centrality". The largest food services are concentrated in the core of the city, including the Downtown, Northwest, and Eastside areas, as well as in the corridors outside of the downtown business district, as the scale tends to be closer to population and employment centers.

In addition, the development of residential areas and consumer demand has significantly stimulated retail investment throughout the city; while the growth in tourism activity has led to growth in the number of restaurants offering full-service and specific dining needs, particularly in downtown Portland.

Themes Exploring for restaurant reviews

Which words frequently appear in restaurant reviews?

We used lemmatized texts to generate a WordCloud showing all reviews texts after text processing. We excluded  some general unimportant words such as specific food, good, really, come, time, etc using stopwards and we set it to only show the top 300 common words. Words such as “atmosphere”, “experience”, “service” shows people care about a restaurant’s ambiance  as much as they do for the food quality and price. Therefore, this implies we can extract keywords about a restaurant’s atmosphere based on review texts.

What about Frequence?


The chart shows word frequency for all of our potential themes. In the x-axis, the words in the same color are different keywords under a theme. We sorted based on the total number of word counts by themes. Liquor, showing in the red appeared to be the most frequent one and it was represented by different types of alcohol so this theme generally refers to bars. Romance, family, student, and tourist are mainly classified by customer’s titles such as “wife/husband”, “girlfriend/boyfriend”, “children”, etc. which differentiates restaurant positioning and their targeted consumer. By contrast, the theme Classy mainly used adjectives such as cosy, trendy, modern, etc. to highlight the atmosphere. Vegan provides a very specific cuisine so we put it alone. Outdoor and music describes a restaurant’s ambience from their supportive facilities; unfortunately, not many reviews commented on this.

And correlation?

Our goal is to select themes that have unique spatial characteristics; in other words, we want to select themes that yield the best cluster results. We know it’s unrealistic to label a restaurant with only one theme based on reviews; however, we can minimize the overlap by the Pearson Correlation. We first calculated theme intensity as the number of selected reviews for a theme divided by all available reviews for a restaurant. Then based on all restaurant’s theme intensity, we see the theme Outdoor is positively correlated (>0.3) to the theme Liquor, it means Outdoor and Liquor select many overlapping reviews, which also means those overlapping reviews often meation “outdoor” and “beer/wine/cocktail,etc” at the same time. Therefore, there’s no need to keep both themes because they refer to very similar restaurant locations so we only kept Liquor because it has more reviews (i.e. higher word frequency).

After checking the word frequency and the Pearson correlation, our final themes are: Classy, Family, Liquor, Romance, and Vegan. To show how our themes are geographically related to ranking scores, we also used the rating stars (1 to 5) from the Yelp Dataset. The animation above made from Leaflet shows that most themes share a common hotspot at Downtown Portland with the waterfront and this is also where most 5-star restaurants are located. Other than that, every theme has their own sub-hotspot dispersed across the city.

Interactive map

cluster result

We employed agglomerative hierarchical clustering of the sklearn package in Python which is one of the most common hierarchical clustering techniques (Pedregosa et al, 2011). Our analysis generates 5 clusters distributed across Portland by using linkage criterion "ward", and the set distance threshold to 5 without connectivity matrix.

The obvious spatial differences and clusters among Portland by the restaurant ambience theme can be identified:
Cluster 0 is dominant in the centre of Portland, with small parts distributed in outer Portland. Cluster 1 tends to group together in the outer edges of Portland. While Cluster 2 and Cluster 3 majorly located in the western part of the city. And the Cluster 4 randomly distributed across the city.

Based on the result of hierarchical clustering we assign each cluster with labels below:
Cluster 0 - Featured vegetarian food, high classy vibe with high stars
Cluster 1 - family gatherings, medium-low stars
Cluster 2 - prominent liquor
Cluster 3 - Highlighted romantic atmosphere
Cluster 4 - high stars

Compared to the other clusters, Cluster 0 has a relatively high level of classy ambience with a high mean star rate 4.0, meanwhile, this group owns many restaurants providing vegan food. The average star rate in Cluster 1 is slightly lower than others, but the vibe is suitable for family gatherings. The Cluster 2 owns widest review rates in general except for the beverages, which has most outstanding performance in the 5 clusters. The Cluster 3 owns the best romantic vibes and Cluster 4 has the highest average restaurant star rate.

Discussion

Our results are helpful for understanding the diverse consumer groups in neighbourhoods of Portland and might potentially attract different client groups (Rahimi, Andris and Liu, 2017). The clustering results also indicate which places have nice bars for nightlife, and which place has good infrastructures but lacking in additional attractions for visitors. For instance, despite that the Forest Park neighbourhood is well equipped with amenities such as markets and shops, along with unique visions of the urban park, the number of restaurants is less than neighbourhoods of centre city and they are less attractive for family groups.

Further Suggestions

Suggestions for Restaurants

It would be conducive for restaurants to redefine the atmosphere more suitable for the restaurant and improve the service quality of the restaurant. For example, for the northeast area of the downtown, it has good commercial infrastructure and public facilities. And this area labelled with high family and low stars. For restaurants in this area, more consideration can be given to setting the atmosphere which is suitable for families to eat together. At the same time, they could focus more on the quality of dishes and service, which may be different from other restaurants in the area and obtain more customers.

Suggestions for City Government

The analysis results of this project will be helpful for the government and company to plan commercial facilities. For example, it could first consider the neighborhood with high romance to built an romance theme park. Because the proportions of couples in the consumer groups are relatively higher in these neighborhoods. Also, it can provide some suggestions for city management. For example, the three neighborhoods labelled with high liquor might have more safety and noise issues during the night because of the danger of alcohol. Thus, it would be better to increase police patrols at night in these three areas.
